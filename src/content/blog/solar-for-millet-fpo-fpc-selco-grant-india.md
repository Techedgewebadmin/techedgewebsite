## Why Millet Processing Is One of the Best Solar Candidates

Millet processing loads align almost perfectly with solar generation:

- **Daytime operations** — grading, de-stoning, milling and packaging runs during daylight hours when solar is generating at full capacity
- **Consistent base load** — motors, blowers and conveyors draw steady power that solar sizing can target precisely
- **High tariff category** — most processing units fall under MSEDCL LT-V or similar industrial categories with effective rates of ₹15–20/unit, making every saved unit highly valuable
- **Strong seasonal variation** — post-harvest peaks (June–November for kharif millets) are months with high solar irradiance in Maharashtra and Karnataka

The match between when solar generates and when a millet FPC consumes power is nearly ideal. You don't need battery storage. An on-grid system with net metering captures the full benefit.

## The SELCO Foundation Grant — What It Is and Who Qualifies

[SELCO Foundation](https://selcofoundation.org) is one of India's most respected clean energy grant bodies. They fund solar access for underserved entrepreneurs, rural enterprises and social businesses — with a particular focus on women entrepreneurs, FPOs and livelihood-linked businesses.

**A SELCO grant can fund 40–50% of your solar system cost.** For a ₹17.64 lakh solar installation, SELCO granted **₹7,64,180** (43%) to Mandprabha FPC — reducing their out-of-pocket cost to ₹10 lakhs and cutting payback from 29 months to **16 months**.

### Who Typically Qualifies

- Farmer Producer Companies (FPCs) and FPOs
- Women-led enterprises in rural areas
- Social enterprises with livelihood and farmer income linkages
- NGO-promoted processing units
- Enterprises in SELCO's mission geography (Maharashtra, Karnataka, Gujarat, Odisha, Rajasthan and more)

### What SELCO Looks For

SELCO's grant assessment is rigorous. They want to see:

1. **Genuine ownership intent** — the enterprise must commit to owning and operating the asset long-term
2. **Financial co-contribution** — typically 50–60% self-contribution from the enterprise's own funds
3. **Mission alignment** — demonstrated impact on farmer livelihoods, women empowerment, or rural food security
4. **Financial viability** — a credible solar ROI model showing the enterprise can sustain operations post-installation
5. **Social impact documentation** — SDG alignment, beneficiary count, income improvement evidence

This is not a form you fill online in 10 minutes. It requires a properly prepared Impact & Investment Assessment Report, billing analysis, ROI model and ownership documentation — all in SELCO's specific format.

## Beyond Electricity — What Solar Does for a Millet FPC

The financial case is compelling on its own. But for an FPC, solar creates three additional strategic benefits:

**1. Cost Certainty for Procurement Planning**
Electricity bills that swing from ₹14,000 (February) to ₹1,01,000 (August peak) make it almost impossible to plan working capital. Solar eliminates the energy charge variability — you know your fixed demand charge, and that's it.

**2. Credibility with Institutional Buyers**
Retailers, cooperatives and agri-enterprises increasingly ask about the environmental footprint of their suppliers. A solar-powered millet processing unit with a documented CO₂ saving (~61 tonnes/year for a 45 kW system) is a meaningful differentiator in procurement decisions.

**3. Farmer Income Protection**
When your FPC is energy-independent and cost-stable, it can offer better and more consistent prices to farmer members. Energy cost volatility is often passed back to farmers in the form of lower procurement prices. Removing that volatility directly protects farmer income.

## Other Grants for FPOs — Beyond SELCO

SELCO Foundation is the most accessible grant for small and mid-sized FPCs, but it's not the only option:

**GIZ (Deutsche Gesellschaft für Internationale Zusammenarbeit):** Funds rural renewable energy projects across India, often in partnership with state agriculture departments and NABARD. Larger grants for cluster-level interventions.

**Gram Vikas:** Supports rural community energy access with a focus on tribal and marginalised communities. Smaller grants but simpler application process.

**NABARD RIDF and SHG loans:** Not grants, but subsidised lending for agri-infrastructure including solar. Interest subvention available.

**State Government Schemes:** Maharashtra's MEDA, Karnataka KREDL, Rajasthan RREC and other state agencies have capital subsidy schemes for FPO solar that can be combined with central schemes.

Navigating these requires knowing which schemes are active, which combinations are permissible, and what documentation each body requires. Techedge assesses all applicable options for your specific FPC before recommending a financing structure.
