Every commercial or industrial site with a diesel generator is paying two prices for backup power: the price of the DG set itself, and the price of running it — fuel, maintenance, and downtime during start-up. Most facilities accept this as the cost of doing business. It doesn't have to be.

A Battery Energy Storage System (BESS) — a hybrid inverter paired with a LiFePO₄ battery bank — can replace a diesel generator outright. No fuel, no noise, no warm-up delay. And on a 5-year total cost of ownership basis, it can already be cheaper than the DG set it replaces, even before adding solar.

---

## The Real Cost of Running a Diesel Generator

Take a typical 60 kVA / 50 kW DG set, sized for a mid-size commercial or industrial facility:

- **Initial cost:** ₹7,00,000
- **Fuel consumption:** ~12 litres/hour at full load
- **Diesel cost:** ₹100/litre (illustrative)
- **Operating cost:** ₹1,200/hour → ₹36,000/month → ₹4,32,000/year

Run that DG set through a normal pattern of daily outages over 5 years, and fuel alone adds up to **₹21,60,000** — more than three times the cost of the generator itself. Add servicing, spares, and AMC, and the total 5-year cost of ownership comes to roughly **₹28,60,000**.

That number rarely shows up on anyone's radar because it's paid a few thousand rupees at a time, at the fuel pump, month after month.

## What a 50kW BESS Costs Instead

A 50kW hybrid inverter with a matched LiFePO₄ battery bank, sized to cover the same load:

- **Initial cost:** ~₹15,73,000 (inverter + battery, installed)
- **Running cost:** grid electricity to recharge the battery — at a typical commercial ESCOM rate of ₹10/unit, charging 50 kWh costs ₹500
- **Operating cost:** ₹500/cycle → ₹15,000/month → ₹1,80,000/year

Over 5 years, that's **₹9,00,000** in running cost — bringing the total cost of ownership to roughly **₹24,73,000**.

**5-year saving: ₹3,87,000 — without solar.** Add rooftop solar to recharge the battery during the day instead of drawing from the grid, and the running cost drops further, shortening payback well beyond this baseline comparison.

## Why the Math Works in the Battery's Favour

The DG set has a lower sticker price, but nearly all of its lifetime cost is in the fuel it burns — a cost that scales directly with your outage hours and only goes up as diesel prices rise. A BESS shifts that same money into the *upfront* system cost, where it stops growing. Once installed, the "fuel" is grid electricity (or free solar), at a fraction of the per-unit cost of diesel.

The other costs rarely make it into a generator's brochure:

- **Fuel logistics** — storage, theft risk, and supply during shortages
- **AMC and spares** — moving parts wear out; batteries with a solid-state BMS don't
- **Noise and emissions** — a real constraint for sites near residential areas or with ESG commitments
- **Start-up delay** — a DG set takes seconds to crank and stabilise; a BESS switches over within milliseconds

## When BESS Makes Sense for Your Site

A straight DG-to-battery swap is the right call when:

- You already run a DG set 2+ hours/day on average due to grid outages
- Diesel, AMC, or spares costs are a recurring budget line you'd rather remove
- Noise or emissions are a compliance or neighbour concern
- You're planning a solar installation and want the battery to double as backup

It's not the right call for sites with rare, short outages where a DG's low upfront cost is genuinely never recovered — a proper load and outage-pattern assessment settles this before you commit either way.
