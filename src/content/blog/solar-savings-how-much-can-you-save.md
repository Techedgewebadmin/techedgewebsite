Every solar salesperson throws around numbers like "save 80% on your electricity bill." But what does that actually mean for your business — a fuel station, a factory, a warehouse, or a cold storage facility?

At Techedge, we've been installing solar systems since 2019. Here's an honest breakdown of what real savings look like.

---

## The Basics: How Solar Savings Work

Solar savings come from one source: **units you generate yourself instead of buying from the grid**.

If your electricity tariff is ₹7.50/unit and your 30 kWp system generates 130 units/day:

- **Daily savings: ~₹975**
- **Monthly savings: ~₹29,250**
- **Annual savings: ~₹3.5 Lakhs**

That's the math. But real savings depend on three things:

1. **Your current tariff** — commercial and industrial users in Karnataka pay ₹7–₹9/unit
2. **Your load hours** — does your business run during the day (solar hours)?
3. **Your system size** — matched correctly to your actual consumption

## The DG Offset — The Hidden Saving

If your business runs a diesel generator during power cuts, solar-hybrid with battery backup can **replace diesel entirely**.

At ₹100/litre, a 30 kVA DG running 4–5 hours/day costs:
- **₹2,000–₹2,500/day** in diesel
- **₹60,000–₹75,000/month**

A properly designed hybrid solar system with battery backup eliminates this cost. This is exactly what we did for a cold storage facility in Mysuru — the DG savings alone halved the payback period.

---

## What Affects Your Payback Period?

- **Net metering approval** — credited units reduce your bill further
- **HPCL / MSME / PM Surya Ghar subsidies** — can reduce upfront cost by ₹1–2.5 Lakhs
- **AIF interest subvention** — for agro-processing units, 3% interest subvention on loans (see our AIF guide)
- **System quality** — Tier-1 panels (like Goldi Solar) degrade less than 0.7%/year; cheap panels can degrade 2–3%/year
