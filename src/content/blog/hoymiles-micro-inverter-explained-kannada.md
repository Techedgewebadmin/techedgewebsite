## String Inverters vs Micro Inverters — What's the Real Difference?

Most rooftop solar in India uses a **string inverter** — one central box that converts DC from all panels to AC at once. It's simple and cost-effective. But it has a fundamental flaw: every panel in the string must perform at the same level. Shade one panel, a tree limb crosses one corner, bird droppings land on one module — the entire string drops to match the weakest panel.

A **micro inverter** solves this at the source. Instead of one central inverter, each solar panel gets its own small inverter mounted directly behind it. Each panel operates with its own MPPT (Maximum Power Point Tracking). Shading one panel has zero effect on the others.

## Why Micro Inverters Make Sense for Indian Rooftops

Indian homes face conditions that string inverters struggle with:

**Mixed orientations** — Many homes have roof sections facing different directions: east, west, flat, sloped. A string inverter cannot optimise across orientations simultaneously. With micro inverters, an east-facing panel and a west-facing panel each run at their own maximum — the east panel captures morning sun; the west panel captures evening sun. Total daily yield goes up.

**Partial shading** — Trees, water tanks, staircase structures, neighbouring buildings. Most urban and peri-urban rooftops in India have some shade for at least part of the day. With a string inverter, a shadow on one panel can reduce output by 30–50% system-wide at peak times. With micro inverters, only the shaded panel underperforms.

**Long-term mismatch** — As panels age at different rates, soiling accumulates unevenly, and string losses compound over 10–25 years. Micro inverters eliminate this: each panel always runs at its own MPPT regardless of what others are doing.

**Expandability** — Adding panels to a string system later requires string recalculation and may exceed the inverter's capacity. With micro inverters, you add one panel and one micro inverter at a time — no redesign, no constraint.

## Is a Micro Inverter Right for Your Rooftop?

A micro inverter system is the right choice when:

- Your rooftop has shading from trees, water tanks, parapet walls or neighbouring structures
- You have panels on multiple roof faces (east + west, flat + sloped sections)
- You want to expand the system in phases — start with 4 panels, add 2 more next year
- You want per-panel fault isolation (one unit fails, the rest keep running)
- You value a longer-warranty, lower-maintenance premium system

A string inverter may be the better choice when:
- Your rooftop is completely unshaded, single orientation — a well-designed string system is highly efficient in this case
- You need a large system above 15–20 kW where commercial-grade string inverters are more cost-effective
- Upfront cost is the primary constraint
