## Project Overview

**Mandprabha Farmer Producer Company** is a women-led millet processing enterprise in rural Satara, Maharashtra — founded and run by Mrs. Arati Abhay Nilakhe (32 years). The company sources millets from smallholder farmers and processes them for distribution, providing a critical livelihood link in Maharashtra's agricultural supply chain.

Electricity was the enterprise's single largest operational cost — running at **₹74,630/month** on the industrial LT-V B II tariff (₹18.60/unit effective rate). Peak months hit over ₹1 lakh. This was directly threatening the enterprise's viability and the income of the farming community it supports.

Techedge was engaged as **the complete solution partner** — from financial advisory and grant application through to solar engineering, installation and ongoing maintenance.

## The SELCO Foundation Grant

[SELCO Foundation](https://selcofoundation.org) is one of India's most respected clean energy grant bodies — funding sustainable energy access for underserved entrepreneurs and communities across India.

Securing a SELCO grant requires detailed mission-alignment documentation, financial modelling, and evidence of genuine ownership intent. **Techedge prepared the complete grant application package:**

- Grant Impact & Investment Assessment Report (SELCO format)
- 12-month MSEDCL billing history analysis
- Solar ROI and payback model
- Social impact scorecard (SDG 7, women empowerment, rural livelihood)
- Asset ownership commitment documentation

**Result: SELCO Foundation grant of ₹7,64,180 approved** — 43% of total project cost.

> *"SELCO's grant acts as a high-leverage catalyst — converting a self-funded but financially stretched entrepreneur into a fully energy-independent, profitable enterprise owner."*
> — Techedge Grant Assessment Report, April 2026
